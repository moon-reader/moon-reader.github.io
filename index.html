Download and install Moon Reader on PC

Moon Reader is built for Android, but Windows users can run it reliably via popular emulators such as Bluestacks, Nox, or LDPlayer. If you’re on Windows 11, you may consider Windows Subsystem for Android (WSA) to run Android apps natively; however, compatibility and app availability vary, so emulators remain the most consistent option.

To begin, install an emulator, sign in with your Google account, search for “Moon+ Reader” on the Play Store, and install. If you want advanced features like PDF tools, TTS, ad-free reading, and reading statistics, you can purchase the Pro version directly from the Play Store within the emulator.

Method 1: Android emulators
  • Install: Download Bluestacks/Nox/LDPlayer from their official sites and install.
  • Sign in: Use your Google account to access the Play Store.
  • Get the app: Search “Moon+ Reader”, click Install, and start reading on PC.

This works for most users due to stability, easy setup, and full feature support for Moon Reader.

Method 2: Windows Subsystem for Android (WSA)
  • Requirements: Windows 11 with WSA enabled.
  • Setup: Enable WSA and install Moon Reader as an Android app.
  • Note: App compatibility and store access are limited, so emulators are usually more reliable.

Use this if you want tighter integration with Windows and lower emulator overhead.

Key features of Moon Reader on Windows

Moon+ Reader supports a wide range of ebook formats: EPUB, PDF, DJVU, AZW3, MOBI, FB2, PRC, CHM, CBZ, CBR, UMD, DOCX, ODT, RTF, TXT, HTML, MHT/MHTML, Markdown, plus images like WEBP and archives such as RAR and ZIP. OPDS is supported for browsing online ebook catalogs, making it convenient to discover and download books.

You can fine-tune display settings: line spacing, font size and weight, drop shadows, justification, alpha colors, fade edges, and more. It includes over 10 built-in themes with quick Day/Night switching. Page-turn options are diverse, and you can map actions to touch gestures, volume keys, camera/search/back buttons, and hardware keys for fast navigation.

Deep customization lets you assign 24 actions to 15 events (tap, swipe, hardware keys, etc.), along with five auto-scroll modes (scroll curtain, by pixel, by line, by page) and live speed control. Brightness can be adjusted via vertical edge gestures. Smart paragraph handling removes extra whitespace, supports proper indentation, and includes “keep your eyes healthy” options for long reading sessions.

Page-turn animations include realistic effects with adjustable speed, color, and transparency, plus five distinct transition styles. The “My Bookshelf” organizes favorites, downloads, authors, and tags; custom covers are supported, and importing/searching books is straightforward. Layout features include margin control, hyphenation, dual-page landscape mode, support for all orientations, and multimedia EPUB3 (audio/video).

For sync and backup, you can back up/restore to cloud via Dropbox/WebDAV and sync your reading position across phones and tablets. Annotations, bookmarks, dictionaries, translation, and sharing are integrated, and the Reading Ruler (six styles) helps you focus on your current line. The app is localized into 40+ languages, including Vietnamese.

The Pro edition adds more: removes ads, TTS (Text-to-Speech, shake-to-read), extra themes/backgrounds/fonts, reading statistics, customizable toolbars, headphone/Bluetooth controls, character renaming and role swapping, multi-touch support, password protection on startup, homescreen shortcuts to books, shareable notes/bookmarks, and bookshelf widgets for quick access.

Regarding “All Files Access,” the app explains it enables reading/managing ebooks across all folders, saving PDF annotations directly into PDFs, and storing books from network libraries, cloud services, and websites to local storage. The “My Files” tool is popular for robust file management and relies on this permission to function fully.

Many users pick Moon Reader on PC for the large-screen comfort, deep personalization, convenient library management, and cloud syncing of bookmarks/notes/progress. While it originates on Android, running it on Windows is mature enough for everyday ebook reading needs.

Optimization tips and best practices

  • Structured library: Organize ebooks by author/series and use tags. “My Bookshelf” and “My Files” make importing fast from your storage folders.
  • Eyes-friendly themes: Choose Day/Night based on room lighting; enable “Keep your eyes healthy” when reading for long periods.
  • Smart auto-scroll: Use scroll curtain or by-line for long documents; tune speed based on content type.
  • Action mapping: Assign gestures/keys for quick access to search, bookmarks, theme switching, navigation, and font size adjustments.
  • Cloud sync: Enable Dropbox/WebDAV to back up and keep your reading position consistent across phone and PC (via emulator).
  • Audio & TTS: In Pro, listen to books via TTS; control playback with headphones/Bluetooth for convenience.
  • EPUB3 multimedia: Use audio/video in supported books for language learning or interactive titles.
  • PDF annotations: If you read PDFs often, Pro lets you save annotations directly into files for portability.
